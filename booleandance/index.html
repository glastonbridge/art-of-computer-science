Boolean Dance

An animation of dance data created during an Art of Computer Science hackday in which ways to visualise computer science concepts were explored.

The animation takes motion captured dance data and draws it according to rules from boolean algebra. For example, 'XOR circles' uses exclusive or logic to draw parts of circles where an odd number of them intersect.

The Art of Computer Science project is devised and run by Genevieve Smith-Nunes of readysaltedcode and has received a Google RISE Award and a Grants for the Arts Award (Arts Council England).

Many thanks to readysaltedcode and decoded for running the hackday. Also to Alex Shaw for creating the motion capture data.

This animation built by Peter Cook using JavaScript, Canvas & bits of D3.
